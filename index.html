It's not just about technology; it's about empowerment, progress, and a continent confidently claiming its place on the global innovation stage.

Past Events

InnovateKenya 2024 - A Landmark Gathering

Last month, Nairobi played host to InnovateKenya 2024, a truly landmark event that brought together over 500 tech enthusiasts, entrepreneurs, investors, and policymakers from across East Africa and beyond. Held at the Kenyatta International Convention Centre from May 15-17, this year's conference exceeded all expectations, showcasing the vibrant pulse of Kenya's burgeoning tech ecosystem. Highlights included a captivating keynote address by Dr. Aisha Noor, founder of AfyaData, who shared her journey in leveraging AI for rural healthcare solutions. The "Pitch Perfect" startup competition saw eight promising ventures vie for seed funding, with "EcoFarm Robotics" taking the top prize for their sustainable agricultural automation. Interactive workshops on blockchain integration and ethical AI development were fully booked, underscoring the community's hunger for practical skills. Beyond the formal sessions, the networking lounges buzzed with energy, facilitating countless connections and potential collaborations. InnovateKenya 2024 truly cemented Nairobi's position as a regional tech hub, leaving attendees inspired and equipped to drive the next wave of innovation.

CodeFest Africa 2023 - Cultivating the Next Generation

Looking back at October 2023, we fondly recall CodeFest Africa, a pivotal event held in partnership with local universities aimed at nurturing the continent's budding coding talent. Hosted virtually with satellite hubs in Nairobi, Lagos, and Johannesburg, CodeFest Africa 2023 saw over 1,500 aspiring developers participate in a challenging yet rewarding 48-hour hackathon. The event focused on sustainable development goals, with teams tackling real-world problems through code. Standout projects included a mobile app designed to connect smallholder farmers with direct markets, and a web platform facilitating peer-to-peer electricity sharing in off-grid communities. Mentorship sessions with seasoned developers from Google Africa and Microsoft Kenya provided invaluable guidance, while the closing ceremony celebrated not just the winners, but every participant's dedication and ingenuity. CodeFest Africa 2023 was more than just a competition; it was a powerful demonstration of how collaborative coding can drive meaningful change and empower the next generation of African innovators. The seeds planted last year are already beginning to bear fruit in various tech ventures across the continent.
